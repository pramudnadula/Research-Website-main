Literature Survey

Sri lanka is the 4th largest tea producer in the world [1]

Figure 1. Annual tea production 1999 - 2022

In recent years, advanced acoustic technologies and frequency analysis have emerged as powerful tools for early pest infestation detection in agriculture [2]. These innovations provide valuable insights beyond traditional methods, showing the potential to revolutionize pest management practices [3]. In this section, we explore research utilizing acoustic methods to detect and assess pest infestations, laying the foundation for their applicability in the context of termite infestations in tea plantations. This research aligns with the Tea Research Institute of Sri Lanka's mission to reduce chemical pesticide use and promote sustainable crop management practices.

Nanda et al. implemented a comprehensive termite detection system in their study [4], integrating acoustic and temperature signals. Their approach demonstrated remarkable accuracy, achieving a detection rate of 97.167% and the ability to predict termite population size with an RMSE of 98.316. However, it's essential to consider that this method is best suited for identifying termite infestations in individual plants, and its equipment requirements may not be ideal for tea plantations.

Research Gap

Following areas are the research gaps found in most of the recent researches.

Identification & classification

Currently, Sri Lanka faces the absence of an intelligent system for the precise identification and categorization of tea pests and diseases. This challenge is primarily attributed to the striking visual resemblances exhibited by diverse tea disorders, posing hurdles in the development of a viable solution.

Severity assessment

Reports have highlighted the development of smart solutions for identifying specific tea pests and diseases. However, these solutions have not yet incorporated assessments of disease severity and the progression level of pest damage.

Information sharing

Developing a real-time communication system is recognized as a top priority to enhance the swift exchange of information among tea growers, extension personnel, and researchers, thereby improving the effectiveness of pest and disease control efforts.

Research Problem & Solution

Proposed Problem

How to classify pest and diseases in tea and provide surveillance to people in real time?

The proposed problem in the tea industry revolves around the challenge of precisely identifying pest infestations, particularly Upcountry Live Wood Termites (ULWT), and accurately assessing their severity within tea plantations. The existing manual detection methods frequently fall short in terms of precision, resulting in delayed responses to infestations and the associated risk of economic losses. This issue underscores the need for an innovative solution, which leverages advanced technologies like Deep Learning to enable real-time monitoring, more accurate identification, and immediate actions to combat pest infestations


Product Demonstration - Solution

Proposed Solution

TeaGuardian, the proposed solution, utilizes Deep Learning to accurately identify pest habitats and assess infestation severity, with a primary focus on Upcountry Live Wood Termites (ULWT) in tea plantations. This innovative system integrates Internet of Things (IoT), Artificial Intelligence (AI), and Machine Learning techniques, which address the limitations of manual pest detection methods. By combining audio detection and image recognition algorithms, TeaGuardian enables real-time monitoring and alerts for ULWT infestations, providing tea growers with crucial information and the ability to take immediate actions against infestations. This advanced technology reduces the reliance on manual detection methods and minimizes the use of harmful chemicals, enhancing the safety and sustainability of tea cultivation and minimizing economic losses. TeaGuardian stands as a promising solution that could revolutionize the tea industry by improving tea quality and ensuring its resilience against pest-related challenges.

Research Objectives

Classify the upcountry live wood termites infestation calculate severity level and potential distribution

The first objective is to create a smart system for early identification and severity assessment of upcountry termite infestations in tea plants. This system will utilize advanced technologies like machine learning, AI, IoT, and GIS to aid tea planters in promptly detecting and responding to termite issues. By doing so, it aims to reduce economic losses and environmental harm caused by chemical pesticides. The system will also help tea planters monitor infestation distribution and severity, enabling them to take preventive measures.

Develop a mobile application to identify Red Spider Mite infestation

The second of this study is to develop a mobile application to identify Red Spider Mite. The intended outcome is to enable workers to identify the infected tea plants in the field more easily and accurately. The application will take a photograph of the adaxial (upper) surface of the suspected tea leaf and determine whether it is infested with red spider mite.

Identifying the Progression Levels of Red Spider mite

Third objective is to create a smart system utilizing Quadrant Technology to accurately assess the severity of red spider mite infestations in tea leaves and bushes. It employs machine learning and image analysis to identify and quantify infestation levels. This early detection is crucial for effective pest control. The system enables farmers to apply precise control measures and prevent further infestation spread, ultimately boosting crop yields and profits.

Identifying dispersion of RSM patterns and forecasting weather

The fourth objective is to forecast the possibility of red spider mite invasions. The complicated ecology of these pests is influenced by several separate parameters, including the humidity, temperature, shade conditions, wind speed, and direction of the wind, all of which are evaluated as part of this forecasting effort. The clever use of a multiple linear regression machine learning model, created to analyze how these elements interact and project the likelihood of red spider mite infections, is used to carry out this prediction task

Methodology

Figure 2. High Level Architecture of the system.

The proposed pest and disease Surveillance system consists of 4 main components. They are;

  1. Classify the upcountry live wood termite’s infestation and calculate severity
  2. To identify Red Spider Mite infestation.
  3. Identifying the Progression Levels of Red Spider mite
  4. Identifying dispersion of RSM patterns and forecasting weather.

The termite identification system begins with users recording audio data from tea plants using a high-quality microphone connected to a Raspberry Pi device. The captured audio is processed to remove noise and then securely uploaded to the cloud. Location tracking is integrated to record the user's geographic coordinates. In the backend, the audio data undergoes preprocessing steps, including resampling, noise cleaning, segmentation, and feature extraction, resulting in detailed frequency graphs. These graphs provide a visual representation of the audio's characteristics over time. The processed audio and frequency graphs are analyzed using advanced techniques like frequency analysis and neural networks (RNN and CNN) to identify termite infestations accurately. Users are promptly notified if no infestation is detected. When an infestation is found, the system calculates its severity using audio amplitude and historical data. The precise location of the infested tea plant is determined through location tracking and displayed on a map. Users receive notifications about the infestation.

Finally, the system visualizes the potential spread of the infestation on the map, helping users make informed decisions to mitigate further damage to their tea plantations.
